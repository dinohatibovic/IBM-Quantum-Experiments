\section{Introduction}

Quantum hardware has reached a level of stability that enables reproducible experiments on mid-scale processors such as IBM Heron R2 (156 qubits) and Heron R1 (133 qubits). This paper documents verified results from eight experiments executed on these devices, covering entanglement fidelity, quantum key distribution, variational quantum circuits, and quantum randomness.

The primary goals of this work are:
\begin{itemize}
    \item to provide fully reproducible hardware results,
    \item to benchmark Heron R2 vs Heron R1,
    \item to validate quantum protocols such as BB84 and Bell entanglement,
    \item to demonstrate statistical rigor in quantum hardware analysis.
\end{itemize}
